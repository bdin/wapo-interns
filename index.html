MP Editor
Annabeth Carlson
University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign
@annabethcarlson

Editing has always been my passion. Anytime a teacher gave out a grammar assignment and the class complained, I secretly felt content while fixing the run-on sentences and incorrect contractions. Not to mention I am always the go-to person for my siblings and friends when they need someone to read their research papers. In high school, I took an introductory journalism course and realized that my love of editing could translate into a future career. In college, I gained more experience through my classes and internships. I worked at The Daily Illini student newspaper and tried almost every job in the newsroom like reporter, assistant features editor, assistant copy chief and night editor. I enjoy writing and editing all types of news, but my biggest passion is producing fashion and lifestyle content.

This year, I was the managing editor for online and oversaw all aspects of our digital presence. Under my leadership, The Daily Illini won best newspaper and best website in the state at the Illinois College Press Association awards. This was pretty cool but the best part of my college paper was that it became my home away from home and I met all of my best friends there. I’ve also had internships at The New York Times and Chicago Sun-Times and can’t wait to copy edit at the Washington Post this summer! In my free time, I love traveling when possible. I spent an amazing semester in Barcelona during my junior year, working on my Spanish and eating all of the tapas in sight. I also love playing tennis, hunting down the best ice cream places and working on my fashion blog (stylebyannabeth.com).

Local
Rachel Chason
Duke University
@rachel_chason

I grew up in Washington and am, a little bittersweetly, a recent Duke graduate. I’ve thought I wanted to be a journalist for a long time — since the fifth grade, to be precise — so when I arrived at Duke, I immediately headed to the office of our school newspaper, The Chronicle, to check it out. I was hooked, and spent my four years as a reporter and editor at the paper. As an underclassman, I also interned at USA Today’s headquarters in McLean, was a correspondent with USA Today College and worked on a structured journalism start-up team in New York. Last summer, as an intern with CNN’s digital politics team, I wrote more than 30 articles and had the opportunity to do everything from contribute to coverage of the conventions to research Trump’s overseas real-estate holdings to create my own Trump mash-up video. I knew I wanted to keep following the presidential campaigns when, near the end of the summer, I dropped a weight on my toe trying to read a push notification with Trump’s latest tweet. So when I got back to school in the fall, I started interning with The Raleigh News & Observer, where I spent the year working on both the politics and metro desks. Because North Carolina’s status as a swing-state meant an unending stream of rallies, I had some really cool opportunities to help with campaign coverage — from interviewing Chelsea Clinton to reporting on Hillary Clinton’s eve of the election rally in Raleigh to covering election night from former Gov. Pat McCrory’s viewing party. I’ve also written about everything from urban beekeeping to hemp production to endangered strawberry crops — and really loved all of it. I couldn’t be more excited to keep learning and to keep doing the reporting that I love at the Post, which is the paper that inspired my love of journalism growing up and continues to do so today.

Hello East Coast! I come to D.C. by way of California where I graduated from UC Davis with a PhD in Plant Biotechnology in June 2017. I like doing science, but I prefer talking about doing science - preferably over beers. After my internship, I’ll begin a quest for the perfect job: a little science doing, a little science teaching, a little science writing, and a whole lot of talking about science — preferably over beers.

Rachel Siegel
Yale University
@rachsieg

The oldest of three siblings, I grew up in Dallas. There, conversations on the news of the day fostered an early curiosity about current events, history, and American politics — from debates on open-carry legislation in Texas to side-of-the-road stops for historical markers. These family influences often extended beyond the home. In one instance, while on a family vacation, my dad paused to explain a memorial recognizing non-Jews who rescued Jews during the Holocaust, inspiring what would become a written collection of profiles honoring 36 of these Righteous Gentiles. A love for writing had actually caught on a few years earlier when I founded my sixth-grade newspaper, which included photocopying the issues myself. Summer internships at the Dallas Morning News earned me my first bylines, before coming to college where I spent four years reporting and editing for the Yale Daily News. My various YDN beats involved coverage of the Yale administration, but one story took me half way around the world to Singapore to report on gay rights activism at Yale’s affiliated campus there. Other internships at The Marshall Project and The New England Center for Investigative Reporting shaped a particular interest in coverage of the American criminal justice system. I hope to one day trace the effects of wrongful convictions on exonerated men and women, and chronicle the stories of communities affected by violent crime, mass incarceration, and poverty. In addition to my reporting interests, I am a lentil enthusiast and a lover of all-things ginger-flavored. Along with every member of my immediate family, I know the complete dialogue from “The Pink Panther” (the Steve Martin version), yet I’m the only one who can claim a similar mastery of most “West Wing” episodes.
